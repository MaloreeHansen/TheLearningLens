About this project

The American people expect to interact with government through digital channels such as websites, email, and mobile applications. By building better digital services that meet the needs of the people that use them, the delivery of policy and programs becomes more effective.

One way to advance smarter digital service delivery is by putting the right processes and practices in place to drive outcomes and accountability:

  • The Digital Services Playbook identifies a series of "plays" drawn from successful best practices from the private sector and government. The plays outline an approach to delivering services that increases flexibility, iteration, and focus on the needs of the people that use those services.
  • The TechFAR Handbook highlights the flexibilities in the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) that can help agencies implement "plays" from the Playbook, with a particular focus on how to use contractors to support iterative, customer-driven software development.

Technical details

The Playbook is compiled from Markdown files using Jekyll. To propose a specific change, submit a pull request with your change to one of the source Markdown files. The Plays are available in the _plays folder.

Running the site locally

  1. Install Ruby and Bundler if not already installed
  2. Fork this repository and clone it on your computer
  3. Navigate to the folder and run bundle install
  4. Run jekyll serve --watch
  5. Visit http://localhost:4000/ in your browser
